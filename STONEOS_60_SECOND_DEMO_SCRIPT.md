# StoneOS — 60-Second Product Demo

**Format:** Screen capture with lightweight motion graphics, confident voice-over, subtle industrial percussion.

| Time | Visual storyboard | Voice-over / audio |
|---|---|---|
| **0–07s** | Fast cuts: paper production notes, WhatsApp messages, spreadsheet tabs and someone searching for a slab number. Red labels appear: **“Scattered data”**, **“No live stock truth”**, **“Delayed decisions.”** | **VO:** “A stone factory can move hundreds of blocks and slabs—while its information stays trapped in paper, messages and disconnected spreadsheets.” **Audio:** Low industrial pulse; notification sounds overlap briefly. |
| **07–14s** | The clutter collapses into the StoneOS sign-in screen. Transition into the **My Work** dashboard and animated factory flow map. | **VO:** “StoneOS replaces that confusion with one secure operating system for the factory.” **Audio:** Pulse resolves into a clean, steady rhythm. |
| **14–25s** | Follow one highlighted material path across the dashboard: **Receive → Cut → Polish → Reserve → Dispatch → Bill**. Each stage updates as the cursor moves through it. | **VO:** “Every physical event is recorded once—from receiving a raw block, through cutting and polishing, to reservation, dispatch and billing.” |
| **25–36s** | Split-screen role views: Operator records B-21 and LPM work; Supervisor verifies operations; Manager sees inventory and commercial workflows; Owner opens Team Access. | **VO:** “Each person sees only the work they need. Operators record production, supervisors keep the flow moving, managers control operations, and owners retain final authority.” |
| **36–47s** | Open a slab record and visually trace it backward to its parent block, cutting session and polishing run, then forward to its sales reservation and delivery. | **VO:** “StoneOS preserves the full material trail, so every slab can be traced back to its block—and forward to the customer.” **On-screen copy:** **“One source of operational truth.”** |
| **47–55s** | Return to the dashboard. Live cards show raw stock, unpolished slabs, finished inventory and active production. A manager sees the next operational action. | **VO:** “Live inventory and production status replace end-of-day guesswork, helping the team act before delays become losses.” |
| **55–60s** | StoneOS logo over the clean factory-flow graphic. End card: **“StoneOS — From raw block to revenue.”** Secondary line: **“Run the factory from one trusted workflow.”** | **VO:** “StoneOS. From raw block to revenue—one trusted workflow.” **Audio:** Short resolved brand hit. |

## Capture notes

- Use the real StoneOS dashboard and workflow screens; avoid generic ERP stock footage after the opening problem montage.
- Keep cursor movement deliberate and let the animated cursor/physics interactions reinforce the sense of live operational control.
- Use anonymized factory, customer and financial data.
- Target **125–135 spoken words per minute** and leave the final logo visible for at least two seconds.
